Project Overview

AdunolaBooks is a literary platform built to publish serialized fiction and poetry the same way modern audiences consume everything else, one chapter, one drop, at a time. Instead of a static blog or a single long PDF, it is built as a living reading experience, with story catalogs, genre tagging, a chapter reader, and a dedicated poetry archive.

This was a full stack build I owned completely, from information architecture and reading experience design to the actual front end and content system that powers it. Every story, chapter structure, and page on the site was designed and engineered by me, then connected to a real content backend so new chapters could be published continuously without rebuilding the site each time.

The Problem

Publishing original fiction and poetry online usually means choosing between generic third party platforms with someone else's branding and ads, or a bare bones blog that does nothing to make a story feel alive. Neither option gave readers a real sense of walking into a library built specifically for these stories.

AdunolaBooks needed to be its own destination, a branded literary world with a homepage that pulls readers into a featured story immediately, a browsable catalog organized by genre, and a chapter by chapter reading flow that keeps people coming back weekly.

Content Architecture

I structured the platform around three connected content types so the library could grow without becoming cluttered.

Stories

Serialized fiction across genres like psychological horror, YA romance and dark fantasy, each with its own cover, genre tags and chapter list.

Chapters

Individual chapter pages with read time, chapter numbering and a "continue reading" flow that carries readers forward automatically.

Poems

A standalone poetry archive for shorter, standalone pieces, separated from the longer serialized fiction catalog.

User Flow

READER JOURNEY
User lands on homepage
  → Featured story hero with cover, genre tags and synopsis
    → Clicks "Start Reading"
      → Enters chapter 1 of the story
        → Reads and clicks through to the next chapter
          → Returns to homepage or browses "More from the Library"
            → Explores Stories or Poems by genre
              → Subscribes for new chapter notifications

Design Decisions

The visual language leans dark and cinematic, closer to a streaming platform than a traditional book site, because the goal was to make browsing stories feel as immersive as browsing a show catalog. Cover art is treated as the primary visual anchor on every card, with genre tags layered on top so readers can self select into the mood they want before committing to a story.

Typography was chosen specifically for long form reading comfort inside the chapter view, while the homepage and browse pages use a bolder, more editorial type scale to create contrast between "browsing" and "reading" states.

Content System & Publishing Flow

New chapters and covers are managed through a connected content backend, which meant I had to design a publishing workflow, not just a front end. This let the story catalog grow weekly with new chapters and new titles without needing a code change for every update, keeping the platform genuinely sustainable as a long term publishing project rather than a one time build.

Outcome

AdunolaBooks is live at adunolabooks.com with an active, growing library of serialized stories and poetry, weekly new chapters, and a subscriber flow for readers who want to stay inside the story. It stands as proof that a genuinely engaging literary platform, catalog, reader, and publishing system included, can be designed, built and run by one person.
