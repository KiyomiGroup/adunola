Project Overview

Kiyomi Group is the studio brand I built to house all of my client and product work under one roof, software engineering, web and app development, and product design. The site needed to do three jobs at once: present the brand as a credible software partner for founders and businesses, showcase the actual products built under it, and sell a set of paid tutorials teaching design, development and AI skills.

I designed the entire brand experience and built the site myself, from the homepage narrative and portfolio system to the "How We Work" process pages, the discovery call booking flow, the lead capture contact form, and the tutorials storefront. Every part of how the business presents itself online was planned, designed and engineered by me.

The Brief

Before this site existed, all of the products I had built, StreetTasker, AndreStylez, CV by Kiyomi, Swappy, Portfolia and TailorCV, had no single home that tied them together as proof of one studio's capability. Potential clients had no clear place to see the range of work, understand the process, or actually start a project. There was also no digital storefront for the tutorial courses being taught.

The requirements were: a homepage that immediately communicates what the studio does and who it serves, a portfolio section pulling in real shipped products, a transparent breakdown of the working process, and a tutorials section that functions as an actual course storefront with pricing and enrollment.

Site Architecture

I structured the site across dedicated pages, Home, About Us, Services, Portfolio, How We Work and Contact, plus a full tutorials hub covering Vibe Coding, AI First UI/UX at three skill levels, Adobe Illustrator, and an AI Mastery course. Each page has a clear single job so visitors are never confused about what action to take next, whether that's booking a call or enrolling in a course.

LEARNER JOURNEY

01

Understanding the idea, users and definition of success.

04

Full testing, launch, and ongoing support after go-live.

Portfolio Integration

The portfolio section pulls together the studio's shipped work into one credible proof-of-capability grid, connecting directly back to the individual products and their own case studies.

Design Decisions

The visual identity needed to read as a legitimate software studio rather than a solo portfolio, so the design leans structured and confident, clear section headers, consistent card systems for both portfolio and tutorial content, and a light and dark mode toggle to match different visitor preferences. Trust indicators like response time, weekly progress updates and "client owned code" are placed prominently because founders evaluating a studio need reassurance just as much as they need to see the work.

Outcome

Kiyomi Group is live at kiyomigroups.com and now functions as the central hub for both client acquisition and course sales, tying together every product in this portfolio under one credible studio brand. It is the clearest proof point of the full range of what I do: not just designing and coding individual products, but building and running the business infrastructure around them.
