About Me

I build systems.
I ship products.

Full stack developer based in Lagos, Nigeria. I turn ideas into live, working applications using modern engineering and AI accelerated workflows.

The Story

I started in design. Figma, wireframes, prototypes. But I kept running into the same wall: great designs sitting in handoff limbo, waiting on developers who had different priorities. So I taught myself to build.

That decision changed everything. I stopped being someone who designed systems and became someone who engineers them into existence. Frontend to backend. Concept to deployed URL. The full picture.

Today I run Kiyomi Groups, an independent build practice out of Lagos. I work with startups, entrepreneurs, and brands who need real products fast. Not mockups. Not decks. Shipped software.

The AI revolution in development is something I've leaned into completely. Claude Code and Cursor aren't shortcuts to me. They're engineering tools that let me build at a pace that traditional cycles simply can't match. I use them intelligently, and the output reflects that.

What I Believe

Good software is invisible. When you build well, users don't think about the tech. They just experience the product. That's the goal on every project I take on.

Design and engineering aren't separate disciplines. They're two lenses on the same problem. I think in both simultaneously, which is why the products I build tend to feel right and work right at the same time.

Speed matters. Shipping a working product in 72 hours teaches you more than six weeks in planning. I bias toward building, measuring, and iterating.
